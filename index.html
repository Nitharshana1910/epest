Introduction

The most significant field in Sri Lanka is agriculture. The agricultural world is facing more difficulties due to crop pests that injure or harm cultivated plants. It is a crucial matter faced by farmers and home gardeners nowadays. Significant challenges to those interested in cultivation are the pest attack and disease. Pests spread the disease, and the yield is decreased. However, it is possible to control pest attacks and infections in the early stage of the attack to reduce pesticide use and keep the farm safe as They are aware of the pest. Mobile applications can provide accurate identification rather than manual detection. Mobile applications and technologies are created when we consider the solution. The importance of the proposed solution is to increase the rate of the plant product and achieve high revenue without any cost. One of the main components which are used in this system is the image processing technique. The images of pests will be taken, and they will be subjected to various preprocessing for noise eliminating and enhancement of the pictures. Furthermore, the user will find the life cycle stage of a particular pest using image processing. The user can identify the stage of the damaged plant by applying the classification algorithm. Users can get aware and predict the kind of pest with the damaged plant using image processing. The content analysis is based on the machine learning process, especially using a Convolutional neural network (CNN). Hence, our system will help to get knowledge of organic pest prevention methods.

Research Scope

There are several researches have been done regarding pest attacks and detections. However, most of the studies rely on image processing, machine learning, and deep learning technologies. In most cases, the pest was detected only based on pest images or plant damages, but works including pest and plant damage and pest lifecycle stage analysis are not usually found. In this literature survey, we have discussed the conclusion drawn from some of those research papers related to the topic.
Machine vision and digital image processing techniques are widely used in the agricultural field, and they have great perspectives, particularly in the field of Pest detection and plant protection. Thenmozhi et al.proposed an automatic insect detection system to identify the crop insects by applying digital image processing techniques using machine vision and image processing techniques. To determine the shape of insects, digital image processing techniques were used to perform preprocessing, segmentation, and feature extraction on photos of crop insects. For the segmentation sobel edge detection was used. In feature extraction, the shape of the pest can be recognized by nine geometric shape features. This insect shape identification method performs with round, oval, triangle, and rectangle shapes. The researcher used MATLAB Toolbox to implement the system. Pratibha et al. have performed an early pest detection system that detects an insect called Borers that affects many plants, including tomatoes. Digital image processing technique was used to detect the tomatoes and identify the border in them.
In the recent years machine learning and deep learning technologies have been chosen and implemented to detect plant pest attacks. For example, Suthakaran et al. has researched the Detection of the affected area and the classification of pests using convolutional neural networks from the leaf images. The system first determines whether the leaf is affected or not and then it calculates the affected are of the leaf in the image. Then classification was performed using convolutional neural networks. Finally, calculating the percentage of the affected area can reveal the seriousness of the attack, which leads to taking the appropriate measures.
Aitor Gutierrez et al. presented the implementation and comparison of two approaches for vision-based pest detection and classification. First, a deep learning solution was compared to a solution that combines both computer vision and machine learning techniques. The study was focused on the most harmful pests of tomato and pepper crops. The result showed that the deep learning technique was better than machine learning and computer vision because it combines disease detection and classification in one step, achieves higher accuracy, and balances speed and accuracy by choosing different models.
Yufeng Shen et al. proposed developing a system for detecting and identifying stored-grain insects by applying a deep neural network. Faster R-CNN (Region-based Convolutional Neural Network has been used to extract areas that might contain the insects in images and classify the insects in areas. An improved inception network was developed to extract feature maps. Excellent results for the detection and classification of these insects were achieved. The test results showed that the developed method could detect and identify insects under stored grain conditions.
Pests can affect the plant’s structures, and yield or create serious human-health concerns. Pest attack may reduce a plant's ability to produce fruits or grains, or pests may feed on the produce directly, thus contaminating human food supplies. When insects cause damage to garden plants, they may become pests in the garden. Damage reflects itself in a variety of ways, depending on how the insect feeds or chews on the plant. There are a lot of research has been done on agriculture area and more generally to detect pests using pests’ images using image processing . They used shape feature, color feature and texture feature extractions to classify and recognize the images of pest. Almost available researches developed with plant’s images to detect the only pest using image processing. This research paper covered color, texture and morphology edges feature extraction of plant to recognize the pest using plant images. Limited number of researches moved with plant’s images to detect the pest but they only focused on damaged leaves using image processing. In every stage it shows different features and behaviors .and the important thing is, in every stage of lifecycle it can be a harmful insect for farming. If we have the knowledge about those stages, we can use various methods to prevent in every stage. Life cycles are analyzed by not only farmers but also agriculture students and who maintains home garden. We are not able to see pests all the time. But the plant damage has stabilized. So, the other best idea for detecting the pest is to consider plant damage.
Provide the knowledge of pests in an easy visual manner and organic methods to prevent pests' impact.
Sub Objective 1: Predicting harmful insects (crop pests) and beneficial insects that affect the plants based on Image Processing.
Sub Objective 2: Detect the life cycle stage of crop pest and show 3D Visualization of predicted crop pest’s lifecycle.
Sub Objective 3: Crop pest prediction based on damages and symptoms of plants using image processing.
Sub Objective 4: Classify the damaged stage of plants and recommend organic prevention methods.
The Proposed system will be carried out under four components.
1. Identify the insect that has affected the plants using image processing
2. Identify the life cycle stage of the pest and show the details of other stages.
3. Identify the pest according to the plant's damage and symptoms using image processing.
4. Classification of the affected level of plant damages using machine learning. Recommend organic prevention methods.
The Machine Learning component of this research will use a convolutional neural network to analyze the images captured via the video device. Once the ML (Machine learning) model has been trained, it will categorize the binary classification results. Python was selected as the programming language, along with the following libraries: NumPy for handling array data of images, OpenCV for image classification, TensorFlow, and Keras to handle the ML (Machine learning) framework.
The following imahe processing steps will be carriedout.
A. Initialization
B. Image preprocessing
C. Image Segmentation
D. Feature Extraction
E. Data Processing and Classification
